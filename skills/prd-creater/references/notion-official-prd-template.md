## Stable structure from official Notion guidance
Notion's official help content says most PRDs share five recurring attributes:
- `Context`
- `Goals or Requirements`
- `Constraints`
- `Assumptions`
- `Dependencies`

Notion's own PRD example then turns that guidance into a working template with these sections:
- `Context`
- `Goal and KPIs`
- `Constraints and assumptions`
- `Dependencies`
- `Tasks`

## Official writing steps that expand the template
Notion's PRD writing guide adds four recurring drafting moves:
1. Define the product and its purpose.
2. Determine goals.
3. Identify assumptions and constraints.
4. Limit the scope of work.
5. List features.
6. Define release criteria.
7. Set metrics for success.

Use these steps to enrich the base five-section template when the user needs a fuller PRD.

## Optional context layers from Notion's Duolingo product spec article
Use these only when they improve clarity:
- `Hypothesis`: Useful for experiments, MVPs, or learning-oriented features.
- `Meeting goals`: Clarify what feedback or decision is needed in the next review.
- `Background and summary of changes`: Explain what changed from previous thinking or iterations.
- `Related work`: Link previous PRDs, research, strategy docs, meeting notes, or tasks.
- `Design and interaction`: Add Figma links, screenshots, or interaction notes.
- `Quantitative feedback`: Break metrics into `Success metrics`, `Feature metrics`, and `Guardrail metrics`.
- `Qualitative feedback`: Capture interview signals, test feedback, or internal reviews.

## Writing rules inferred from the official material
- Put enough context near the top so stakeholders do not have to hunt across tools.
- Keep the document lightweight but link-rich; PRDs are often one-page docs that point to deeper evidence.
- Make goals measurable and pair them with metrics.
- Use dependencies to reveal what must be true before launch.
- Turn tasks into status-friendly items that can be moved into a Notion board if needed.
- When information is missing, mark it explicitly instead of hiding the gap.
